## 1.0 GENERAL

The StarGate 5 is a PoE+ native BACnet® intrusion detection panel offering true open connectivity for
diverse applications. Designed to communicate with any ANSI/ASHRAE 135-2010 compliant system or
security central station, the panel enables seamless integration of intrusion detection, security, and
building management systems.
The StarGate 5 controller supports 8 I/O points, expandable in sets of 8 additional points. The panel can
be installed directly or housed within a metal enclosure with a 24VDC power supply. The unit can also
provide local battery back-up charged by the local power supply or PoE+ supply.
In addition to traditional PIN-based arming/disarming, the StarGate 5 supports a SAML-based user
authentication process, allowing customers to centrally and uniquely manage who can arm/disarm their
facilities. Via an integrated LCD touchscreen, users are prompted to enter a unique User Name/Password
combination, which is validated against a centralized SAML server. This process provides the highest level
of IDS authentication and security.








Advanced SAML user authentication

## 6.0 INSTALLATION PROCEDURES

## 6.1 SELECTING A LOCATION

Determine a suitable installation location for the StarGate 5 controller that is secure and provides an
environment with temperature ranging from no less than -40 degrees C and no greater than +70 degrees
C with a relative humidity no greater than 93% non-condensing @ +40 degrees C.

## 6.2 POWER SOURCE

Determine power source:
a. PoE+ with at least 25.5 Watts available – must meet IEEE802.3at specification.
b. Local DC supply rated at 24VDC nominal +/-25% rated at 40 Watts minimum, power cable length
not longer than 30m (98.5 feet)

![StarGate 5 image 210](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_210.png)

## 6.3 MOUNTING THE PANEL/CONTROLLER

Install the StarGate 5 controller onto a suitable surface using the mounting holes located along the long
edges of the board. There are 10 mounting holes in total.
Note: mounting hardware to be supplied by the installer.

![StarGate 5 image 211](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_211.png)

![StarGate 5 image 212](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_212.png)

Alternatively, determine the appropriate conduit knockouts on the DAQ enclosure and remove. Then
mount the enclosure to the mounting surface using the installer-provided hardware. Using the mounting
holes along the long edges, mount the StarGate 5 controller to the appropriate location on the enclosure
backplane.

![StarGate 5 image 213](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_213.png)

## 6.4 TERMINATING TAMPER-PROTECTED INPUTS

All tamper-protected inputs must be terminated using either:
a. A 2K termination resistor block (DAQ Part# R07-09771) wired with the black leads to the input
device and white leads to the panel. Note: resistor packs not included.

![StarGate 5 image 214](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_214.png)

b. Or with two 1K Ohm resistors supplied by the installer***.***

![StarGate 5 image 215](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_215.png)

Resistors should be installed at the sensor and not on the StarGate 5 terminal blocks.
Tamper protected inputs are monitored for four states:

Sensor Normal

Sensor Alarm

Tamper circuit shorted out

Tamper circuit cut open

## 6.5 OUTPUT RELAYS

Output Relay contacts are rated at 10A. Each I/O point can provide 50mA @12.5VDC +/- 10% for
powering a sensor.

![StarGate 5 image 216](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_216.png)

## 6.7 RIOX 5 EXPANSION

RIOX-5 input/output expansion modules are used to increase the point count of StarGate 5 controllers,
enabling the integration of additional StarWatch components and sensors. Through direct, on-board
connections, up to seven RIOX-5 units can be added to a single StarGate 5, with each module providing
eight additional status inputs and eight relay outputs.
All inputs are tamper protected and can detect normal, alarm, tamper open, and tamper short conditions
on the monitored line. LEDs are utilized to indicate the signal condition and can be used for diagnostic
purposes. Software configuration allows for normally open or normally closed sensor input contacts to
represent the normal condition. Outputs are provided with relays from Form C contacts, with link
selection allowing outputs to be configured as normally open or normally closed.

## A.0 APPENDIX

## A.1 OPTION TO PROVIDE POWER TO TOUCHSCREEN FROM AN AUXILIARY SUPPLY

For touchscreen keypads exceeding the maximum recommended distance from the StarGate 5 panel,
power can be provided from an external source.

The power supply must provide between 8 and 28 VDC.

The power consumption of the touchscreen is 7 watts.

Connect the communications (CAT5) cable from Port 3 of the Stargate 5 to either RJ45 socket
on the DAQ interface board, J2 or J3 as normal.

![StarGate 5 image 382](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_382.png)

![StarGate 5 image 383](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_383.png)


Connect the auxiliary power supply to the two-position terminal block, TB1. In order to prevent
back-feeding power from the StarGate 5 into the power supply, a 3-position terminal block
with a diode is provided.

![StarGate 5 image 384](./images/star-gate-5-installation-guide-rev-006_384.png)


Connect positive voltage into position 3 on the terminal block.

Bridge position 3 to position 1 using the supplied diode. Connect the cathode of the diode to
position 1, the positive input of TB1.

Connect ground to the middle position 2.

If the voltage of the auxiliary power supply is greater than the voltage of the StarGate 5 (14V),
a diode is not required, and the positive lead of the power supply can be directly connected to
Pin 1 of the connector.
